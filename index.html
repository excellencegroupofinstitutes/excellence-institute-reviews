About the Excellence Institute Review Portal

The Excellence Group of Institutes review portal is the official feedback channel for current students of Excellence Institute, Ludhiana. From this page you can post a faculty review covering teaching style, teaching methods, and overall classroom experience — across tuitions, the computer centre, internship courses, and higher‑secondary streams (+1 and +2 Science, Commerce and Arts).

Reviews submitted here are completely anonymous. The Excellence review form does not record your name, email, phone number, or device identity. The Excellence Group administration uses these reviews internally to recognise strong faculty work and to improve where students feel learning could be better — they are never published on a public page.

  • Anonymous — no personal data is captured.
  • Open to all current Excellence Institute students across primary, middle, secondary and higher‑secondary classes.
  • Covers tuition faculty, computer course instructors, and internship‑course mentors.
  • Used solely to improve teaching quality and the overall student experience at Excellence Group of Institutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Excellence Group of Institutes review portal?

It is the official feedback portal for students of Excellence Group of Institutes, Ludhiana. Students use it to post honest, anonymous reviews of their faculty members so the institute can continuously improve teaching quality.

Are reviews on this page really anonymous?

Yes. The Excellence Institute review form does not collect your name, email, phone number, or any personal identifier. Reviews are stored only with the rating, comment, class, and faculty selection.

Who can post a review on Excellence Group of Institutes?

Any current student of Excellence Group of Institutes can post a review when an active review session is open. Tuition students, computer centre students, and internship course students may all submit feedback.

How are faculty reviews used at Excellence Institute?

Reviews are read internally by the Excellence Institute administration to identify strengths and areas for improvement in teaching style, methods, and the overall student experience. They are never shared publicly.
